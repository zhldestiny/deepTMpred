Introduction

   Transmembrane proteins (TMPs) are essential for cell recognition and communication, and they serve as important drug targets in humans. Transmembrane proteins' 3D structures are critical for figuring out their functions and drug design but are hard to determine by some experimental methods. Although some computational methods have been developed to predict transmembrane helices (TMHs) and orientation, there is still room for improvement. Considering that the pre-training language model can make full use of massive unlabeled protein sequences to obtain latent feature representation for TMPs, we proposed DeepTMpred, which uses pre-trained self-supervised language models called ESM, convolutional neural networks, conditional random fields for alpha-TMP topology prediction. Compared with the current state-of-the-art tools on a non-redundant dataset of TMPs, DeepTMpred can demonstrate the superior predictive performance at th F1 score of TMH segments. Furthermore, due to the inclusion of latent information from the pre-trained language model, our model could also obtain pretty reliable prediction results for TMPs lacking sufficient evolutionary information.
